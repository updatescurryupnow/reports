Ordering pattern — what to control from the store's own order sheet (P6 invoices)

P6: 49 invoices, $23.99K. Sysco ($8.7K over 5 orders), Modesto ($4.8K/11), Viks ($3.2K/4), APP ($2.3K/4), SJ Distributors ($2.3K/9).

What the ordering data shows

  • Sysco is ordered in heavy single drops, up to $2,629 each on only 5 orders. On a smaller-volume store that is a lot of product landing at once and is a likely source of waste.
  • APP is already close to a good pattern (4 orders, $117 to $906) but inconsistent. Order it as often as needed through the week, keeping each order at the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees, so you buy only what is needed each time.
  • Modesto is ordered in 11 small drops ($185 to $726). Modesto delivers up to three times a week, so frequent small orders here are actually good budget control, not a problem. Keep ordering only what is needed each time.

What to improve

  • Order APP as needed through the week at the $350 minimum (to avoid delivery fees), sized to actual need rather than pre-buying the week.
  • Right-size Sysco. Move from large single drops to two smaller orders a week matched to forecasted sales, so product does not sit on a lower-volume store.
  • Keep Modesto and SJ Distributors as frequent small orders. Both can deliver multiple times a week, so order only what is needed each drop rather than stocking up.
  • Order only what the next two to three days need; Palo Alto's softer volume punishes over-ordering fastest.

Findings for Bibhuti

1
Labor is the leak.
Budget prime ran 65.4% (P4), 57.0% (P5), 60.3% (P6); actual P&L was 64.2% in P4 and 55.9% in P5. Labor sits around 34-36% against a 30% goal, and OT was over the 10-hour allowance in 8 of 13 weeks, peaking near 24.7 hours. Food is closer to target than labor.
2
Lowest guest scores in the network.
Ovation is the network's lowest at 3.7, and WeBox box-meals are the lowest at 3.68. Recurring guest themes are order accuracy, undercooked chana, food not hot, and gravy too salty. These are execution-consistency issues under speed pressure, not recipe problems.
3
3PD error rate is worsening three periods running.
Voosh inaccuracy went 0.43% to 0.96% to 1.48%, the only store getting worse three periods in a row, with Missing Item the top issue. A simple expo discipline fixes the bulk of this.
4
Meal violations are a compliance exposure.
Palo Alto logged 12 meal-break violations across the window against a zero policy. Every missed break is premium-pay liability and documentation risk; it needs to go to zero.

Bibhuti's 4-week plan to get the budget under control

Prime from 60.3% (P6) toward 55%, led by labor discipline; lift Ovation off the 3.7 floor by closing the accuracy and doneness gaps.
Week 1
Reset labor and breaks
Rebuild the schedule to bring labor under 32% and OT to the 10-hour allowance. Build meal breaks into the schedule as fixed slots and track them daily; target zero violations from week one. Post daily labor target vs actual.
Week 2
Expo and accuracy discipline
Install the order-accuracy SOP: KDS-done only when made correctly, item name on each box, check off on the receipt while confirming the full order. Add a drinks/sides/modifier line to the expo checklist to stop the Missing-Item trend.
Week 3
Fix consistency
Add a doneness and temperature check at the pass (chana fully cooked, food hot at handoff) and set a seasoning standard so gravy salt is consistent. Stage and verify every catering pickup against the ticket before the guest arrives.
Week 4
Hold and verify
Re-audit: labor under 32%, OT within allowance, zero meal violations, 3PD errors trending back under 1%. Review the Ovation and WeBox trend with the team and set P7 targets.
