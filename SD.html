Ordering pattern — what to control from the store's own order sheet (P6 invoices)

P6: 26 invoices, $29.2K. Sysco ($16.5K/9), SJ Distributors ($4.8K/6), APP ($4.3K/4), Manneri ($3.1K/3).

What the ordering data shows

  • Ordering is the cost leak here: food runs near 30% against a 21% goal. Sysco alone is $16.5K with single drops up to $2,459, so the volume is concentrated in big stock-up orders.
  • APP drops are large: 4 orders averaging $1,085 each ($890 to $1,249). These are well above the $350 minimum, meaning you are buying a lot per drop. Order as needed through the week, each order at the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees, and only what is needed.
  • Few vendors, big tickets. The pattern is stock-up ordering, which ties up cash and risks waste on a campus store with a seasonal calendar.

What to improve

  • This is the store where ordering control matters most. Cap the weekly order guide to forecasted sales and reconcile mid-week to pull the second order down. Target food toward 24 to 26%.
  • Order APP as needed through the week, each order at or above the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees, sized to need. Stop the $1,000+ single drops unless a confirmed event justifies it.
  • Break the big Sysco drops into smaller, more frequent orders so product does not sit, especially around the academic-calendar dips.
  • Order only what is needed; this single change is the easiest prime recovery on any store.
  • Modesto and SJ Distributors can both deliver multiple times a week, so small frequent orders from them are good budget control. Order only what is needed each drop; no need to consolidate or stock up on these two.

Andrea's 4-week plan to get the budget under control

Prime from 57% (P6) toward 52%, almost entirely by pulling ordering from ~30% toward 24-26%.
Week 1
Cap ordering to sales
Set the weekly order guide to forecasted sales with a hard ceiling, and do a mid-week inventory reconciliation to adjust the second order down. Target food/COGS toward 26% then 24%.
Week 2
Hold the response discipline
Keep the response loop that drove the turnaround: answer Ovation and reviews within hours. Add table or receipt QR prompts to grow written-review volume.
Week 3
Quality consistency
Add a quality and consistency check at the pass to reverse the Uber Eats 3.67 quality slide; spot-check delivery packaging and portioning.
Week 4
Hold and verify
Re-audit: food/COGS trending under 26%, labor steady in the mid-20s, prime toward 52%. Keep using your scheduling as the zero-meal-violation model for the network.
