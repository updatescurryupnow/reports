Ordering pattern — what to control from the store's own order sheet (P6 invoices)

P6: 79 invoices, $35.9K. Sysco ($11.1K/10), Modesto ($7.5K/16), Total Wine ($4.9K/5), Viks ($3.8K/4), APP ($3.0K/6).

What the ordering data shows

  • Modesto comes in 16 small drops ($49 to $1,035). Modesto delivers up to three times a week, so frequent small Modesto orders are good budget control. Keep them, just make sure each drop is only what is needed for the next day or two.
  • APP at 6 orders ($97 to $734) is over-frequent and uneven; the $97 order is below an efficient drop. Order as needed through the week, each order at the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees.
  • Sysco at 10 orders is frequent with steady sizes ($859 to $1,445), which is healthy. Keep Sysco and Modesto frequent; the line to tighten is APP.

What to improve

  • Order APP as needed through the week, each order at or above the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees. Replace the six uneven orders, including the $97 one that incurs fees, with right-sized need-based drops.
  • Keep Modesto and SJ Distributors as frequent small orders. Both deliver multiple times a week, so small frequent drops are the right way to control budget here. Order only what is needed each time.
  • Keep Sysco cadence but order to forecasted sales so the steady drops match demand.
  • Order only what is needed for the next few days; San Mateo's prime swings with how tightly ordering tracks sales.

Nisha's 4-week plan to get the budget under control

Prime held near 52% with labor stabilized, zero meal violations, and the cross-contamination issue closed.
Week 1
Stop the meal violations
Build breaks into the schedule as fixed, named slots and verify them every shift. Target zero from week one. This is the headline compliance fix for San Mateo.
Week 2
Flex labor mid-week
Coach the intra-week adjustment: read actual sales mid-week and cut or add labor on the back half to hold labor near 30%. Post the daily target vs actual so the swing from 25% to 37% does not repeat.
Week 3
Close food safety and accuracy
Confirm and document the veg/non-veg separation SOP and re-train the line. Add a mobile-order alert routine so tickets start on receipt, not at pickup, and a pre-handoff completeness check.
Week 4
Hold and verify
Re-audit: zero meal violations, labor steady near 30%, cross-contamination SOP confirmed, prime back toward 52%. Compare the booked P&L to the tracker so the P4-style gap does not recur.
