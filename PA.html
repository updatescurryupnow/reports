Budget control — P7 ordering and labor in one place · spend as little as possible to close the period

The goal is the period, not the week. Palo Alto has run 3 weeks at 22.7% ordering and 35.1% true labor. With the period already over budget, hitting the weekly goal in W4 is not enough. The question is how far under goal the last week can realistically run.
P7 cannot be fully recovered, so the job is to spend as little as possible from here. Landing on budget would need W4 at 20.0% ordering and 6.5% true labor, which is not operable. Do not just coast at the goal in the final week. Run under it. Ordering at 20.0% and true labor at 26.0% is a stretch, not a fantasy: stores in this network have run ordering as low as 11.8% and true labor as low as 20.5% in a real week.

That squeeze takes $616 out of the period and lands prime at 54.9% (+4.9pt) instead of 55.9% (+5.9pt) if the store simply runs the week at goal.
