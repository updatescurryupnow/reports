Ordering pattern — what to control from the store's own order sheet (P6 invoices)

P6: 42 invoices, $31K. Sysco ($12.0K/6, drops up to $2,584), APP ($5.3K/6), Viks ($3.9K/6), plus internal transfers.

What the ordering data shows

  • Food runs near 30% against a 21% goal, and the booked P&L prime (69%) was far above the tracker, so ordering control is the priority. Sysco drops are very large (up to $2,584) for event-driven demand.
  • APP at 6 orders ($577 to $1,568) is over-frequent and lumpy; the $1,568 drop is a big single buy. Order as needed through the week, each order at or above the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees, sized to the confirmed event schedule.
  • Demand is schedule-driven with zero sales on Sundays, so ordering must follow the confirmed event calendar, not a flat weekly habit.

What to improve

  • Order to the confirmed event schedule, not last week's volume. With zero-sales Sundays, a flat weekly order over-buys.
  • Order APP as needed through the week, each order at or above the $350 minimum to avoid delivery fees, sized to events. Replace the lumpy $577 to $1,568 swings with right-sized drops.
  • Cap big Sysco drops; split into smaller orders tied to specific events so product does not spoil between gigs.
  • Pull food toward 25%; this is the largest actual-vs-tracker prime gap in the fleet and ordering is the lever.
  • Modesto and SJ Distributors can both deliver multiple times a week, so small frequent orders from them are good budget control. Order only what is needed each drop; no need to consolidate or stock up on these two.

Josh's 4-week plan to get the budget under control

Close the actual-vs-tracker prime gap by pulling ordering toward 25% and capping event-day OT, with zero meal violations.
Week 1
Build breaks into event-day staffing
Schedule fixed meal-break slots and relief coverage for every event so breaks are taken even on busy days. Target zero violations from week one. This is the headline fix for Trucks.
Week 2
Cap event-day labor and OT
Set a labor cap per confirmed event and a hard OT approval rule so big days do not balloon. Staff to the confirmed schedule, not an optimistic one.
Week 3
Tighten ordering to the calendar
Order to the confirmed event schedule, not last week's volume, and account for zero-sales Sundays. Pull food/COGS from ~30% toward 25%.
Week 4
Hold and verify
Re-audit: zero meal violations, OT controlled on event days, food/COGS toward 25%, and compare the booked P&L to the tracker so the large P4-P5 gap closes.
