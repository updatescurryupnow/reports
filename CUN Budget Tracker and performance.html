13 weeks · Mar 23 – Jun 21, 2026  ·  Budget + Marquii + Voosh + Ovation + Catering

The one finding. Across every platform and all three periods, almost no negative feedback is about the food. It is about fulfillment: accuracy, timing, recovery, and the phone. The kitchen is producing quality; the handoff is where guests are lost. On budget, only San Francisco runs prime at target. Every other store is over, in one of two ways: ordering-driven (San Diego, Trucks) or labor and OT-driven (San Jose, Palo Alto, Oakland).

How prime cost is calculated. Prime % = (Ordering spend + Labor spend) ÷ Actual Sales, for each period. Ordering % and Labor % are each that spend ÷ sales, so Prime % is simply Ordering % + Labor %. Period figures are dollar-weighted across the weeks in the period, not an average of weekly percentages. Goal is 51% (Food 21% + Labor 30%).

Three SOPs that fix most of it

1
Order-accuracy SOP at every store. Mark the item KDS-done only when it is made correctly; write the item name on each box; then check it off on the receipt while confirming the full order is complete before handoff. Kills the missing-item and customization-error pattern network wide.
2
Expo checklist with a drinks, sides and modifier line. Targets the rising customization errors (Mango Lassi, ThumsUp, sides) that replaced missing-item errors over three periods.
3
Zero meal violations, OT to allowance. 77 meal violations across the window is live CA legal exposure. San Diego (0) is the template. Enforce per-store OT weekly.
